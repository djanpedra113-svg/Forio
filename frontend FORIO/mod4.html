Technical Controls

Technical controls are security measures used to protect systems and data from threats. Examples of this include firewalls, encryption and antivirus software. Preserving the data integrity of digital evidence is an essential aspect of forensic readiness, which is why when investigators collect data they use hashing algorithms such as MD5, SHA-1, SHA3-256, SHA-256 to create a unique digital identifier of the file or device. Hash algorithms are helpful to investigators because even if a single bit data changes the hash value will also change. This allows investigators to compare and analyse the hash values to prove whether or not any alterations have been made which is essential for maintaining legal admissibility. Write blockers are also used during evidence collection to prevent any modifications of physical media devices while the data is being copied. Technical controls ensure that network devices are kept secure, one of the ways that technical controls are implemented is by putting in place effective logging and monitoring procedures. NIST recommends using a range of tools such as SIEM systems, intrusion detection systems and antivirus software to monitor and analyse network behaviour and alerts continuously.

Firewall

A firewall helps block unauthorised network traffic and protects systems from external threats.

Encryption

Encryption protects sensitive information by changing data into a secure format that cannot easily be read by attackers.

Digtal evidence

Digital evidence is any data that is stored, received or transmitted electronically and is admissible in court. Examples of digital evidence include emails, log files, images, documents, internet history and database records.

Chain of Custody

Chain of custody, forms a chronological and permanent digital paper trail which ensures that evidence is admissible in court, secure and has not been altered in any way from the discovery of the threat onwards.
