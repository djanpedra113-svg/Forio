Preparing for Investigations

One of the ways organisations prepare for digital forensic investigations is by creating policies and procedures before incidents happen. Preparation allows organisations to respond more effectively when security breaches occur. One important preparation method is an incident response plan which summarises the steps staff should follow during a cyber incident.

Staff Training

Staff training is important as it helps reduce mistakes that could damage evidence, as well as helping employees understand how to recognise and report suspicious activity. Preparing for investigations involves assigning clear roles and responsibilities to staff members, as well as providing practice with tools used to spot vulnerability.

Logging and Monitoring

Logs are records that are generated by IT systems and track digital activities. The aim of logs is to capture actionable information so that if an incident occurs, organisations can build an accurate timeline of events. Monitoring logs establishes normal patterns for user activity and data transfers so that discrepancies stand out. Organisations use logs to record system activity and logs can help investigators understand; who accessed the system, when they accessed it, and what actions they took.

Risk Assessments

Risk assessments help organisations identify potential threats and vulnerabilities before an incident happens. The first step to creating a risk assessment is defining the scope of your assessment, collecting data that aligns with the scope, providing a risk rating to all potential vulnerabilities and lastly is fully performing the risk assessment.
