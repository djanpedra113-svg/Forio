Policies, legal frameworks and standards

Organisations must follow policies, laws, and standards when handling digital evidence and investigating cyber incidents. Ensuring that effective forensic readiness policies are in place, means that organisations can reduce cost, time and operational impact of investigations. Furthermore, it ensures that they stay within legal and regulatory guidelines, ultimately improving cyber resilience. Policies provide guidance on acceptable use of systems, evidence handling, security procedures and data protection. It is important that forensic readiness policies align with legal frameworks recommended by the GDPR as well as implementing standardised frameworks such as ISO or flexible framework guidelines like NIST. Frameworks such as ISO 27001, must adhere to data protection laws and help to provide structured guidance on policies, risk assessments and continuous improvements.

GDPR

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a law designed to protect personal data and privacy. Organisations must handle personal information securely and responsibly and follow the rules and regulations set within the GDPR in order to be GDPR compliant. Failure to adhere to this law, could result in large financial fines as victims of data breaches have the right to uncapped compensation.

ISO 27001

The framework ISO 27001, is an international standard for information security management. It helps organisations improve security practices and manage risks effectively.

Importance of Legal Compliance

Organisations that follow laws such as the GDPR, securely gather, preserve and analyse digital evidence without tampering and are admissible in court are legally compliant. This is important as it ensures that organisations can properly protect themselves in the event of an incident and not face any legal issues.

Failing to follow legal frameworks results in:

  • Financial loss
  • Loss of reputation
  • Legal action
  • Loss of evidence credibility

Organisations must ensure evidence is collected and stored correctly during investigations.
