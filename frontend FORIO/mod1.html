What is forensic readiness?

Forensic readiness is defined as an organisation's ability to accurately identify, collect, store and analyse digital evidence. Forensic readiness is a critical component of cybersecurity, as it ensures that organisations are prepared to support digital investigations and act accordingly in the event of a cybersecurity incident without disrupting business operations.

What is digital evidence?

Digital evidence is any data that is stored, received or transmitted electronically and is admissible in court. It is important that digital evidence is authentic, has not been altered and is collected in a standardised, scientifically valid method. Some of the goals of providing authentic digital evidence include; maintaining the chain of custody which creates a paper trail of who accessed the data, maintaining legal compliance and reducing business downtime.

Digital Evidence:

  • Emails
  • Log files
  • Documents
  • Internet history
  • Database records

Why is forensic readiness so important?

As technology continues to develop, more organisations are facing an increase in cyber threats leading to legal issues, data loss, reputational damage and financial shortfall. Therefore organisations must be prepared to investigate incidents properly and preserve evidence that may be needed for legal or disciplinary purposes.

What are the benefits of forensic readiness?

  • Faster investigations
  • Reduced investigation costs
  • Better incident response
  • Improved security awareness
  • Stronger legal compliance

Organisations that are not prepared may lose important evidence or struggle to identify what happened during a cyber incident.
