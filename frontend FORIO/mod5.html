Best Practices

Best practices are techniques and methods that organisations should use to minimise risks and produce the best results.The best practices that would be recommended to organisations, would be regular training programmes and simulations exercises for staff. Organisations that ensure that their staff are trained and their knowledge is kept up to date, guarantee that staff are confident in evidence handling and chain of custody requirements. It would also be recommended that organisations prioritise improving logging, monitoring, time synchronisation, documentation and backups. It is vital that organisations have secure and well structured logs that are easy to understand, as this ensures that reconstructing cyber incidents and identifying malicious activity is made simpler and is legally compliant.

Regular Monitoring

It is vital that organisations monitor their systems regularly as it helps organisations identify suspicious behaviour quickly.

Backups

Backups are copies of important data that can be restored after incidents like cyber attacks or system failures.

Documentation

Organisations should document:

  • Security procedures
  • Incident response plans
  • Investigation processes
  • Evidence handling steps

Staff Awareness

Employees should have regular cybersecurity and forensic readiness training to improve knowledge and awareness as well as reduce human error.

Reviewing Procedures

Forensic readiness plans and policies should be reviewed and updated regularly to ensure they remain effective.
